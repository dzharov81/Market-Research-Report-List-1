Some common types include Ammonium Lauryl Sulfate, Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS), Dimethicone, Citric Acid, Polyethylene Glycols (PEG), Sodium Chloride, Formaldehyde, Retinyl Palmitate, and Others. Ammonium Lauryl Sulfate and Sodium Lauryl Sulfate are surfactants that help create lather and cleanse the hair. Dimethicone is a silicone-based ingredient that adds smoothness and shine to the hair. Citric Acid is used to adjust the pH level of the shampoo. Polyethylene Glycols (PEG) are used as thickeners and emulsifiers. Sodium Chloride is a thickening and viscosity-controlling agent. Formaldehyde is a preservative ingredient. Retinyl Palmitate is a derivative of Vitamin A used for its antioxidant properties. The "Others" category refers to additional ingredients used in shampoo formulations. Overall, these ingredients play specific roles in the effectiveness and performance of shampoos.</p></p>
