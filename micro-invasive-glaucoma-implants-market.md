<p><strong>The Micro Invasive Glaucoma Implants Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Main Stream</li><li>Side Stream</li><li>Micro Stream</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Micro-invasive glaucoma implants (MIGS) are minimally invasive surgical devices used to treat glaucoma. They can be categorized into three types: main stream, side stream, and micro stream. Main stream implants include the iStent, which is inserted into the trabecular meshwork. Side stream implants, like the Hydrus Microstent, are inserted into the canal of Schlemm, improving aqueous drainage. Micro stream implants, such as the Xen Gel Stent, create an alternate drainage pathway by connecting the anterior chamber to the subconjunctival space. These implants aim to decrease intraocular pressure and mitigate glaucoma symptoms.</p></p>
