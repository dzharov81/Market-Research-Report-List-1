<p><p>Specialty surfactants are chemical compounds with surface-active properties used in various industries. They can be broadly classified into four market types. Anionic surfactants have a negatively charged hydrophilic group and are commonly used in cleaning and laundry detergents. Cationic surfactants have a positively charged hydrophilic group and are used in fabric softeners and hair conditioners. Amphoteric surfactants can function both as anionic and cationic surfactants, making them versatile for different applications. Other specialty surfactants include nonionic surfactants, silicone surfactants, and fluorosurfactants, each serving specific purposes in industries like agrochemicals, personal care, and paints/coatings.</p></p>
