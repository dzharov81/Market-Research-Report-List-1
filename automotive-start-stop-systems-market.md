<p><p>Automotive start-stop systems refer to technology that automatically shuts off the engine when the vehicle is stationary, such as at traffic lights, and restarts it when the driver releases the brake pedal. Type I start-stop systems are simpler and less expensive, as they use a conventional starter motor and basic control mechanism. In contrast, Type II start-stop systems employ a more advanced starter system, typically an electric motor, and a sophisticated control unit, allowing for smoother and quicker engine restarts. Type II systems are generally considered more efficient and reliable but are also costlier.</p></p>
