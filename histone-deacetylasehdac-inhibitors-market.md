<p><p>Histone Deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitors are a class of compounds used in epigenetic therapy, primarily for cancer treatment. There are several types of HDAC inhibitors, including hydroxamic acids, cyclic tetrapeptides, benzamides, electrophilic ketones, and aliphatic acids. Hydroxamic acids, such as vorinostat and panobinostat, are the most widely used HDAC inhibitors. Cyclic tetrapeptides, like romidepsin, also have potent HDAC inhibitory activity. Benzamides, such as entinostat, target specific HDAC isoforms. Electrophilic ketones, represented by chidamide, covalently interact with HDACs. Lastly, aliphatic acids like valproic acid possess HDAC inhibitory effects, although they have additional mechanisms of action.</p></p>
