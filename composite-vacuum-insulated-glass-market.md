<p><p>Composite vacuum insulated glass (CVIG) is a type of glass that is used for energy efficient glazing applications in buildings. It consists of two or more glass panes separated by a vacuum space with a thermally efficient composite material, such as fiberglass or plastic, to provide excellent thermal insulation.</p><p>The CVIG market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for energy-efficient glass solutions in buildings, along with the growing emphasis on reducing carbon emissions, is driving the market growth. CVIG offers superior thermal insulation compared to traditional double or triple glazed windows, leading to energy savings and reduced heating or cooling costs. Additionally, its ability to reduce condensation and noise transfer further enhances its popularity.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the CVIG market is segmented by end-use industry, including residential and commercial buildings.
