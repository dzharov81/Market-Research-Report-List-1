<p><p>Technical films can be classified into two market types: degradable film and non-degradable film. Degradable films are designed to break down and decompose over time, reducing environmental impacts. They are often used in applications such as packaging or agricultural films. Non-degradable films, on the other hand, do not naturally degrade and can persist in the environment for extended periods. They are commonly utilized in products like industrial films or protective coatings. Understanding the characteristics and applications of both degradable and non-degradable films is crucial in addressing environmental concerns and choosing appropriate film solutions in various industries.</p></p>
