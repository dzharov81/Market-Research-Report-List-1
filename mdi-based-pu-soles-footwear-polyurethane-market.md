<p><p>MDI-based PU Soles (Footwear Polyurethane) are a type of polyurethane used in the manufacturing of footwear soles. They are produced by combining Methylene Diphenyl Diisocyanate (MDI) or Toluene Diphenyl Diisocyanate (TDI) with Polyols. MDI is a type of diisocyanate that provides excellent abrasion resistance and durability to the soles, while TDI is known for its flexibility and cushioning properties. Polyols act as a reactive ingredient that helps in forming the polyurethane structure. These market types play a vital role in the production and performance of PU soles in the footwear industry.</p></p>
