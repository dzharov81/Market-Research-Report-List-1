<p><p>Industrial clay, also known as clay minerals or industrial minerals, refers to a group of minerals composed primarily of hydrated alumina silicates. This growth is primarily driven by the increasing demand for industrial clay in the construction industry, as it is used in the production of bricks, tiles, sanitary ware, and other construction materials.</p><p>Moreover, the growing use of industrial clay in the automotive industry for applications such as molds, casting, and sealants has further fueled market growth. The rising demand for lightweight vehicles and the need for precision manufacturing have increased the adoption of industrial clay in the automotive sector.</p><p>Additionally, the paper industry represents a major application area for industrial clay. With the rising demand for paper products worldwide, the demand for industrial clay as a filler and coating material has surged. <p><p>The industrial clay market consists of various types of clays such as kaolin, smectite, illite, chlorite, and others. Kaolin clay is known for its high heat resistance and is widely used in ceramics, paper, and paints. Smectite clay has excellent swelling properties and is used in drilling muds, agriculture, and cosmetics. Illite clay is valued for its absorbent properties and is used in the production of cat litter and absorbents. Chlorite clay is used in the production of cement, while other types of clays find applications in construction, refractories, and rubber industries.</p></p>
