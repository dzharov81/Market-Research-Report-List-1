<p><p>The lab-grown diamond market comprises two prominent types: CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) diamonds and HTHP (High Pressure High Temperature) diamonds. CVD diamonds are created by introducing carbon-rich gases into a controlled chamber, which form diamond layers on a substrate. On the other hand, HTHP diamonds are formed by subjecting carbon to intense pressure and high temperature in a lab setting, mimicking the natural diamond formation process. Both types offer an ethical and sustainable alternative to mined diamonds, appealing to consumers seeking responsibly sourced gemstones.</p></p>
