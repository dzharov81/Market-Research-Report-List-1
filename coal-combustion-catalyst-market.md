<p><p>Coal combustion catalysts can be classified into two types: solid and liquid. Solid coal combustion catalysts are typically in the form of powdered or granulated substances that are added directly to the coal during combustion. They work by enhancing the combustion process, improving efficiency, reducing emissions, and maximizing energy output. Liquid coal combustion catalysts, on the other hand, are usually added to the coal as a solution or suspension. These catalysts also aim to improve combustion efficiency, reduce pollutant emissions, and increase overall power generation. Both types of catalysts play a crucial role in optimizing coal combustion processes.</p></p>
