There are two main types: silicone-based TIMs and non-silicone TIMs. Silicone-based TIMs are widely used due to their excellent thermal conductivity, low cost, and ease of application. They are commonly used in applications such as CPUs, GPUs, and high-power LED modules. Non-silicone TIMs, on the other hand, offer advantages like lower thermal resistance and improved stability at high temperatures, making them suitable for aerospace, automotive, and military applications where higher performance is required.</p></p>
