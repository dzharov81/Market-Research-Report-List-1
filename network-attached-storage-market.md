On-Premise NAS refers to storage devices that are physically located within a company's premises, providing direct control and accessibility to data. It offers reliable data security and faster access speeds but requires maintenance and upfront investment. On the other hand, Cloud-Based NAS involves storing data on remote servers accessed through the internet. It offers scalability, cost-effectiveness, and remote accessibility, but data security may be a concern, and access speeds can be affected by internet connectivity.</p></p>
