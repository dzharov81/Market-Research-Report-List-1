<p><p>The fire truck market consists of three main types: conventional fire trucks, elevating fire trucks, and special fire trucks. </p><p>1. Conventional fire trucks are the most common type and are used for general firefighting purposes. They come equipped with hoses, pumps, and water tanks, allowing firefighters to combat fires efficiently.</p><p>2. Elevating fire trucks, also known as platform trucks, have a hydraulic platform that can be raised to provide high-level access during emergencies. This enables firefighters to rescue people from tall buildings or carry out firefighting operations from an elevated position.</p><p>3. Special fire trucks serve specific purposes such as hazardous material response, airport firefighting, or forest firefighting. These trucks are designed with specialized equipment and capabilities to handle specific emergency situations efficiently.</p></p>
