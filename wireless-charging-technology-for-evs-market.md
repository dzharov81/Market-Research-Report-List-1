<p><p>Wireless charging technology for EVs involves three types of markets: electromagnetic induction, magnetic resonance, and magneto-dynamic coupling. Electromagnetic induction utilizes a primary coil to generate a magnetic field that induces an electric current in a secondary coil, allowing for efficient charging. Magnetic resonance uses resonant coils to wirelessly transfer power between the primary and secondary coils, enabling charging over longer distances. Magneto-dynamic coupling involves the transfer of electrical energy through rotating magnets, thereby eliminating the need for physical contact between the EV and charging infrastructure.</p></p>
