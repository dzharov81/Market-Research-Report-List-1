<p><p>Solar salt is a type of salt that is obtained through the evaporation of seawater or underground brine. </p><p>The sea water market involves the collection of seawater in large ponds or pans, which is then exposed to sunlight and wind for evaporation, leaving behind salt crystals. This method is commonly used in coastal areas and relies on the natural process of evaporation.</p><p>In the underground brine market, saltwater is extracted from underground reservoirs and brought to the surface. The brine is then evaporated using solar energy, resulting in the formation of salt crystals. This method is mostly utilized in regions with underground saltwater sources.</p><p>Both the sea water and underground brine markets harness solar energy to evaporate water and obtain salt, making them eco-friendly and sustainable salt production methods.</p></p>
