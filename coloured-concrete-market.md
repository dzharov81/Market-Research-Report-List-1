<p><p>The colored concrete market consists of two types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic colored concrete is produced by adding pigments or dyes to the concrete mixture. These pigments are chemically manufactured and allow for a wide range of color options. On the other hand, natural colored concrete is achieved by using natural materials, such as mineral pigments, which are sourced from the earth. These natural pigments produce more earth-toned colors and are often desired for their organic and eco-friendly attributes. Both types offer diverse options for aesthetically pleasing and durable colored concrete applications.</p></p>
