<p><p>Roughness and contour measuring instruments are used to assess the surface texture and shape of objects. Contact instruments involve physical contact with the surface being measured, such as stylus profilometers, which use a stylus to trace the surface. Non-contact instruments, on the other hand, use optical or laser technology to measure surface roughness or contours without making direct contact. These include techniques like white light interferometry and laser scanning, providing accurate measurements without any potential damage to the surface being assessed.</p></p>
