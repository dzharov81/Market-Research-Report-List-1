<p><p>Silicon Carbide (SiC) Ingot is a key component in the production of semiconductors and electronic devices. The market for SiC Ingot can be classified into two types: N-type and Semi-insulated type. N-type SiC Ingot refers to the ingot with added impurities that provide free moving electrons, making it suitable for creating electronic devices like diodes and transistors. On the other hand, Semi-insulated SiC Ingot has a higher resistivity, making it useful for high-frequency applications, such as radio frequency transistors and microwave devices, where isolation and low power consumption are crucial.</p></p>
