<p><p>Polygalacturonase is an enzyme used in various industrial processes, especially in the food and beverage industry. There are two main types of Polygalacturonase called Endo Polygalacturonase and Exo Polygalacturonase. Endo Polygalacturonase breaks down the internal bonds of polygalacturonic acid, leading to the degradation of pectin. On the other hand, Exo Polygalacturonase cleaves off monosaccharides from the end of polygalacturonic acid chains. Both types play crucial roles in various applications, such as juice clarification, wine production, and fruit ripening, where pectin degradation is required.</p></p>
