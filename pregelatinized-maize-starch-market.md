<p><p>Pregelatinized Maize Starch, also known as pre-cooked corn starch, is a modified form of corn starch that has been chemically or enzymatically processed to improve its functionality. It is widely used in various industries, including food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and animal feed.</p><p>In the food and beverages industry, pregelatinized maize starch is used as a thickening agent, stabilizer, and texturizer in various products such as soups, sauces, bakery products, and dairy products. It provides improved texture, viscosity, and stability to these products. In the pharmaceutical industry, it is used as a binder in tablet formulations and as a disintegrant in capsules. In the cosmetics industry, it is used in the formulation of creams, lotions, and powders, as it enhances their stability and texture. In the animal feed industry, it is used as an energy source and binder in the production of pellets and extruded feeds.</p><p>The pregelatinized maize starch market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.5% during the forecast period. Additionally, the rising trend of clean-label and natural ingredients in various industries is driving the demand for pregelatinized maize starch derived from non-GMO corn. However, the availability of alternative starches and the fluctuating prices of corn may restrain market growth to some extent.</p><p>Overall, the pregelatinized maize starch market is anticipated to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing demand for functional ingredients in various industries.</p></p>
