<p><ul><li>Polarization Dependent Optical Isolator</li><li>Polarization Independent Optical Isolator</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Micro-Optic Component Optical Isolators are devices used to control the direction of light propagation within optical systems. They can be categorized into two types: Polarization Dependent Optical Isolators and Polarization Independent Optical Isolators. The former allows only one specific polarization of light to pass through, while blocking the other. The latter, on the other hand, allows light of any polarization to pass through without affecting its direction. Both types find applications in various industries, such as telecommunications, fiber optic networks, and laser systems.</p></p>
