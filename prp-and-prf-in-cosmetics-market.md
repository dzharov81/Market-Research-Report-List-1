<p><p>PRP and PRF are commonly used in the cosmetics market for their rejuvenating and regenerative effects. Pure-PRP refers to platelet-rich plasma that has been extracted from the patient's own blood, containing a concentrated amount of growth factors. Leukocyte-rich PRP, on the other hand, contains white blood cells in addition to platelets, offering potential anti-inflammatory properties. Pure-PRF, or pure platelet-rich fibrin, is obtained by placing a blood sample in a centrifuge to form a fibrin clot, rich in platelets and growth factors. These market types cater to different needs and preferences of individuals seeking cosmetic treatments.</p></p>
