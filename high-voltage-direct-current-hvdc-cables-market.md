Mass Impregnated Cables are made by filling the cable with an insulating fluid to enhance its performance. Extruded Cables are created by extruding the insulating material around the conductor. Underground cables are employed to transmit electricity within cities, avoiding the need for overhead lines and reducing visual impact. Overhead cables, on the other hand, are used for long-distance transmission, stretching over land and connecting power generation plants and distribution networks.
