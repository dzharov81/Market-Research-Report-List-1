<p><p>Herbicide intermediate market types include Sulfonylurea, Imidazolinones, Tebuthiuron, Quinclorac, and others. Sulfonylurea herbicides are widely used due to their effective control of broadleaf weeds, but they can hinder crop growth if not used properly. Imidazolinones are known for their selectivity against grass weeds, allowing for effective control in certain crops. Tebuthiuron is commonly used to control woody vegetation and brush in non-crop areas. Quinclorac is effective against crabgrass and certain broadleaf weeds, making it valuable in lawns and other turf areas. Other herbicides in this market offer various unique properties and target specific weeds.</p></p>
