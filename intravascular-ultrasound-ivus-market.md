<p><p>Intravascular Ultrasound (IVUS) is a medical imaging technique that uses a catheter with a miniaturized ultrasound probe to visualize the interior of blood vessels. It provides high-resolution, real-time images of the vessel walls, plaque buildup, and blood flow dynamics, allowing physicians to assess cardiovascular diseases such as atherosclerosis and guide treatment decisions.</p><p>The IVUS market has been witnessing steady growth in recent years. Factors such as increasing incidence of cardiovascular diseases, rising geriatric population, technological advancements, and growing preference for minimally invasive procedures are driving the market growth. The market is also benefiting from the increasing adoption of IVUS in research and clinical trials.</p><p>According to the provided information, the IVUS market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.4% during the forecast period. One of the latest trends in the market is the development of advanced imaging systems with enhanced features such as better image quality, improved spatial resolution, and faster data acquisition. These advancements help in accurate diagnosis and treatment planning.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the introduction of intravascular imaging systems with integrated fractional flow reserve (FFR) functionality. This integration enables simultaneous assessment of both plaque buildup and the functional significance of coronary artery lesions. Such integrated systems offer convenience and efficiency to healthcare providers, thereby driving their adoption.</p><p>Geographically, North America holds a significant share in the IVUS market due to the presence of well-established healthcare infrastructure, a high prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, and favorable reimbursement policies. However, the Asia-Pacific region is expected to witness the highest growth during the forecast period, attributed to the increasing healthcare expenditure, rising awareness about advanced imaging techniques, and a large patient pool.</p><p>In conclusion, the IVUS market is expected to experience substantial growth in the coming years, driven by various factors including technological advancements and increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases.
