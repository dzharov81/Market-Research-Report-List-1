<p><p>The antimicrobial plastic polymer market includes two main types of additives: inorganic antimicrobial additives and organic antimicrobial additives. Inorganic antimicrobial additives are typically based on metals like silver, copper, or zinc, which inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi on the plastic surface. Organic antimicrobial additives, on the other hand, use biocides or chemicals like triclosan or quaternary ammonium compounds to achieve antimicrobial properties. Both types of additives serve to reduce the spread of bacteria and improve cleanliness in various applications of plastic materials.</p></p>
