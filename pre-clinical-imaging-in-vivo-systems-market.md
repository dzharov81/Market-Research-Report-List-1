<p><p>Pre-clinical imaging systems refer to the use of non-invasive imaging techniques for studying living organisms in pre-clinical research. Optical imaging systems use light to visualize and analyze biological processes. Nuclear imaging systems involve the use of radioactive tracers to detect and study molecular processes. Micro-MRI utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images. Micro-ultrasound utilizes high-frequency sound waves to capture images. Photoacoustic imaging combines ultrasound and laser-induced optical contrast to visualize tissues. Magnetic particle imaging uses magnetic nanoparticles to create high-resolution images. Other systems include positron emission tomography and single-photon emission computed tomography.</p></p>
