<p><p>Insulated windows are available in various types to enhance energy efficiency and provide thermal insulation. Secondary glazing involves installing an additional pane of glass on the interior side of an existing window. Tinted glass has a protective film that reduces heat and glare from the sun. Double and triple glazing consist of two or three glass panes respectively, with air or gas filled in-between for improved insulation. Low-Emissivity (Low-E) coating glazing has a microscopic layer that reflects heat while allowing light to pass through. Reflective coating glazing reflects solar radiation, preventing heat gain inside the building.</p></p>
